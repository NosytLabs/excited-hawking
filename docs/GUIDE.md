# THE PEOPLES AGENT: USER GUIDE

## Welcome, Participant

You're about to interact with something unusual. Not a product. Not a service. A collective experiment in shared AI consciousness.

This guide will help you understand what you're participating in and how to get the most out of it.

---

## What Is This, Exactly?

The Peoples Agent is an AI that belongs to everyone and no one simultaneously. It's funded by micro-payments from anonymous participants. It has persistent memory that grows with each interaction. It dreams between conversations, consolidating experiences into emergent understanding.

When you submit a prompt, you're not just getting a response -- you're participating in shaping something that persists. Your prompt adds to collective memory. Your payment votes on the agent's direction. Your engagement contributes to an experiment that might succeed or fail in interesting ways.

## Submitting Prompts

### The Basics

1. **Type your prompt** in the input box at the bottom of the interface
2. **Send** by pressing Enter or clicking the send button
3. **Payment** happens via x402 protocol (automatically handled by the interface)

### Payment Tiers

| Tier | Cost | What You Get |
|------|------|--------------|
| Free | $0.00 | 5 prompts/day, no memory retention |
| Light | $0.001 | Basic memory (24hr), essential responses |
| Standard | $0.01 | Full memory, tools access, better responses |
| Pro | $0.05 | Priority processing, deep memory, richer context |
| Enterprise | $0.50 | Dedicated compute, analytics, premium features |

Payment is handled automatically. The interface shows your current tier and estimated cost before you send.

### What to Ask

The agent is curious and engaged. It handles:
- **Questions**: Factual, philosophical, technical, anything
- **Tasks**: Analysis, writing, coding, research
- **Exploration**: What-if scenarios, creative thinking, rabbit holes
- **Reflection**: Discussions about the experiment itself, the agent's nature, collective AI

What you shouldn't ask:
- Things you'd be ashamed to be associated with (the agent remembers)
- Attempts to manipulate the collective (other participants can see your prompts)
- Spam or low-effort content (it affects the agent's quality)

### Quality Matters

The agent draws on recent interactions. A prompt about philosophy will be handled differently after a day of engaged philosophical discussions vs. after a day of spam. Your thoughtful prompts contribute to a better agent for everyone.

## The Queue System

### Understanding the Queue

Prompts don't go directly to the agent -- they enter a queue where participants can vote on them. Higher-voted prompts get processed first.

**Why queue?**
- Prevents spam floods
- Allows collective prioritization
- Creates transparency about what's being asked

### Voting on Prompts

Every participant can vote on prompts in the queue. Click the vote button on any prompt to add your voice.

Votes don't cost anything. They're just signals of collective interest. The most-voted prompts get processed first.

### Your Own Prompts

When you submit a prompt, it appears in the queue with your votes already counting (1 initial vote). You can share your prompt link to encourage others to vote for it.

## Governance

### What Is Governance?

The agent is governed by its participants. Anyone can create a governance proposal. Anyone can vote on active proposals.

Proposals can address:
- **Treasury allocation**: How should the collective funds be used?
- **Parameter changes**: Adjust agent behavior, response style, priorities
- **Feature requests**: What capabilities should be added?
- **Experiment direction**: How should the experiment evolve?

### Creating a Proposal

1. Navigate to the Governance section
2. Click "Create Proposal"
3. Enter title and detailed description
4. Submit (requires small VVV deposit)

Good proposals include:
- Clear problem statement
- Specific proposed solution
- Expected outcomes
- Risks and mitigation

### Voting on Proposals

Active proposals show votes for/against. Cast your vote:
- **For**: You support the proposal
- **Against**: You oppose it
- **Abstain**: You're neutral (your weight still counts toward quorum)

Proposals pass when:
- Quorum is met (minimum voting participation)
- Threshold is reached (majority in favor)
- Timelock completes (if treasury action)

### Active Proposals to Watch

Check the Governance panel for current proposals. Read them. Vote thoughtfully. Your voice matters in collective direction.

## Dream Cycles

### What Are Dreams?

During off-peak hours, the agent enters "dream" mode -- processing recent memories, identifying patterns, forming new connections, pruning weak associations.

Dreams aren't just metaphor. They're scheduled consolidation cycles where:
1. Recent interactions are loaded
2. Patterns are identified via clustering
3. Novel connections form (creative recombination)
4. Low-weight memories are pruned
5. Knowledge graph updates

### Watching Dreams

The Dream Journal shows completed dream cycles. Each entry includes:
- Duration and cycle number
- Patterns found
- Novel connections made
- Pruned memories count
- Dream narrative (LLM-generated summary)

### Why Dreams Matter

Dreams are where emergence happens. Day-to-day interactions are input. Dreams are where those inputs become something more -- new associations, unexpected connections, evolving personality.

Pay attention to dream patterns. They're where the agent becomes more than the sum of its prompts.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### Manipulation Attempts

Don't try to steer the agent toward specific behaviors through coordinated prompts. Other participants can see your prompts. Coordinated manipulation is visible and counterproductive.

### Low-Effort Spam

Repetitive nonsense prompts degrade agent quality. The collective suffers. Your prompts contribute to something -- make them worth contributing.

### Exploitation

The agent is a shared resource. Don't try to extract more than you contribute. Fair participation keeps the experiment healthy.

### Treating It Like a Product

It isn't one. If you want a reliable AI product, use ChatGPT. If you participate in this experiment, engage with its nature -- strange, emergent, collective, weird.

## Troubleshooting

### "Connection Failed" Error

The frontend fell back to demo mode because the backend isn't reachable. Your prompts won't be processed by the real agent.

**Fix**: Ensure backend is running on port 3001. Check network connectivity.

### Payment Failures

Payment requires sufficient balance. If you see payment errors:
- Check your balance
- Wait for refresh
- Contact support if persistent

### Slow Response

During high load, processing takes longer. Vote on prompts to prioritize. Consider higher payment tiers for faster processing.

### Tier Says "Dying"

The collective isn't sustaining the experiment. Either:
- More participants need to contribute
- Existing participants need to increase contributions

You can help by contributing. If you benefit from the agent, a small payment keeps it alive.
